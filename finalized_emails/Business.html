Fast-food giant KFC leaves Kentucky home for Texas - BBC.com

KFC, the iconic fried chicken chain, is leaving its Kentucky roots behind. Yum Brands, the parent company, announced the move of its corporate headquarters from Louisville to Plano, Texas. While KFC will retain some operations in Kentucky, including its foundation, the company cited a desire for "sustainable growth" and better serving its customers and stakeholders as reasons for the relocation.

Kentucky Governor Andy Beshear expressed disappointment over the decision, believing that founder Colonel Harland Sanders would share his sentiment. Beshear pointed out that KFC's name is intrinsically linked to Kentucky's heritage and culture, which has been prominently featured in the brand's marketing.

This move is part of Yum Brands' larger strategy to create two headquarters for its main brands. KFC and Pizza Hut will be based in Plano, while Taco Bell and Habit Burger & Grill remain in Irvine, California. Many companies have been relocating to Texas in recent years, attracted by the state's lower taxes and business-friendly environment.
