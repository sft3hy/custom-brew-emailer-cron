Microsoft’s Majorana 1 chip carves new path for quantum computing - Microsoft

Microsoft has announced Majorana 1, the world's first quantum chip built using a novel Topological Core architecture. This breakthrough leverages topoconductors, a new type of material that can produce stable and scalable qubits, the building blocks of quantum computers.

Majorana 1 utilizes unique Majorana particles to achieve greater qubit stability and control, making it more resistant to environmental disruptions. This digital control method simplifies quantum computing and paves the way for scaling to a million qubits, a crucial milestone for tackling complex real-world problems.

This advancement positions Microsoft to provide solutions in areas like materials science, healthcare, and energy by enabling the design and optimization of materials and molecules that are currently beyond the reach of classical computers. The company envisions a future where quantum computers can assist in developing self-healing materials, breaking down microplastics, and optimizing enzyme behavior for improved food production.

Blue Ghost moon lander lowers its orbit to fly closer to the lunar surface (video) - Space.com

Firefly Aerospace's lunar lander, Blue Ghost, has successfully entered lunar orbit and is preparing to touch down on the moon's surface on March 2nd. After a four-day journey from Earth, Blue Ghost completed a crucial engine burn, lowering its orbit to 75 miles above the moon. Firefly's team is meticulously analyzing data and fine-tuning the lander's descent in anticipation of the landing.

This mission, known as "Ghost Riders in the Sky," is part of NASA's Commercial Lunar Payload Services (CLPS) which aims to utilize private companies for moon exploration. Blue Ghost carries ten NASA experiments and technologies designed to study the lunar environment, contributing valuable data to the Artemis program's goal of returning astronauts to the moon.

Launching alongside Blue Ghost was Japan's ispace lunar lander "Resilience," which is also aiming for a soft landing but with a different path and a timeline extending to June. If successful, Blue Ghost will become the second private mission to land on the moon, paving the way for further commercial lunar exploration.

Webb telescope spies the Milky Way’s black hole constantly ‘bubbling’ with light - CNN

Astronomers using the James Webb Space Telescope have witnessed impressive displays of light flares near the supermassive black hole at the center of our galaxy, the Milky Way.

These flares, both short bursts and longer, brighter flashes, occur constantly and appear to be random in nature, making for a dynamic and unpredictable observation. The flares are believed to originate from the accretion disk – a swirling disk of gas and dust – that orbits Sagittarius A* (Sgr A*), the Milky Way's central black hole.

This ongoing observation provides valuable insights into the behavior of black holes and how they influence their surroundings. Astronomers hope to gather more data to understand the mechanisms behind these flares and the black hole's overall activity.
